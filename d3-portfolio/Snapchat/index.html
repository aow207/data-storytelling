Snapchat Politics

Nearly $3.5 million was spent on political ads on Snapchat in 2019.

By Andrew Williams

Published Oct. 29, 2019

Snapchat has released its updated political and advocacy ad report for 2019. This dataset contains information for political ads that are or have been served on Snapchatâ€™s advertising platform. Most ads overwhelmingly target the teenagers and young adults who make up the bulk of users on the social media platform.

In total, the United States has by far the most political ads. With nearly 1,200 ads targeting Americans, the country has an 800 ad lead over the second place contender. A majority of ads are also ran in English followed by French and Norwegian.

$3,493,335 was spent on political advertisments in total, but it appears that there is very little difference in impressions for an ad that costs $60,000 and one that costs nearly double. In fact, the most successful ad on the platform was purchased for only $63,966 and reached 72.5 million people.

It is unclear how Snapchat decides which ads to show users, but it doesn't seem to be based on the amount paid by an organization.

This data also revealed that women are the preferred audience for political advertisments on Snapchat. 72.3% of ads are geared toward women while only 27.7% indicate men as their target audience.
